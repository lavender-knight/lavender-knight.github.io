Nintendo EPD

     Nintendo Entertainment Planning & Development Division is the largest department at Nintendo. It oversees the development of several games and as an artist, I see such beautiful artistry & attention to details coming from this division. Unlike GameFreak (another division of Nintendo), EPD's games actually function on Nintendos scrappy console. (I have a lot of negative feelings about the Switch that don't need to go here)

The Legend of Zelda

     Zelda has been such an influential series in gaming overall with almost every title being on the Artistically Influential Games by Year chart (see history) The two titles that stand out to me the most (probably because I've been in the gaming 'scene' at the time of their release) are Breath of The Wild & Tears of The Kingdom. They are visually unique from most modern games that try to go for a realistic style. Taking on a more anime/cartoony style which, to my knowledge, hadn't been done as well as BOTW's 2017 release. Their lighting engine works extremely well for making the characters look like they're in a cell-shaded style, rather than realistic lighting which can make the models feel flat and unfinished. Not only graphically but this game (BOTW & TOTK) is artistically very beautiful taking place in an open world where you can just go explore and you want to explore because it's just beautiful.

Animal Crossing

     Though Animal Crossing:New Horizons isn't technically that impressive it does have a lot of interesting things going on artistically that I think is part of the reason it blew up so much in popularity during the 2020 COVID-19 lockdown. The designs of all the villagers & objects that you can place are intentionally made to be very cutesy and rounded to fit with the style of the game. There's also some interesting things that the developers did with the camera in New Horizons. You can learn more about this in Shesez's boundary break video .
